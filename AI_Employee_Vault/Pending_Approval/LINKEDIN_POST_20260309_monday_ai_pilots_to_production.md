The gap between "AI pilot" and "AI in production" just got a lot smaller.

Gartner predicts 40% of enterprise applications will include task-specific AI agents by end of 2026. And the data backs it up — over half of surveyed organizations already have autonomous assistants live in production.

But here's what separates the companies seeing real ROI from the ones stuck in pilot purgatory:

They stopped asking "Can AI do this?" and started asking "What's the workflow AI should own?"

The shift isn't about replacing people. It's about identifying the repetitive, multi-step processes that drain your team's time — and letting AI agents handle the coordination.

Three things I'm seeing work for businesses making this transition:

1. Start with one high-volume, rule-based workflow (not your most complex one)
2. Measure before and after — processing time, error rates, team bandwidth freed up
3. Build governance in from day one, not as an afterthought

The companies that get this right aren't just saving costs. They're freeing their best people to do their best work.
