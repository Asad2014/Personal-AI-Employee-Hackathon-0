🚀 Just completed the Silver Tier of the Personal AI Employee Hackathon — and I have to say, this has been one of the most rewarding builds of my career!

💡 What did I build? An autonomous AI Employee that runs 24/7 and handles real work without constant supervision.

Here is what it does:

📧 Monitors Gmail for important emails and drafts intelligent replies
💬 Watches WhatsApp Web for urgent messages via Playwright automation
📝 Auto-generates and publishes LinkedIn posts (yes, this post was routed through it!)
🧠 Creates structured Plan.md files for complex multi-step tasks
📤 Sends emails directly through a custom MCP (Model Context Protocol) server
🔒 Enforces a human-in-the-loop approval workflow for sensitive actions like payments over $100 or communications to new contacts
⏰ Runs on a cron schedule so it survives reboots and stays always-on

🛠️ The tech stack: Claude Code as the reasoning engine, Obsidian as the local-first knowledge base and dashboard, Python watchers as the sensory layer, and MCP servers as the hands that take action.

🎯 The key insight? The best AI systems are not fully autonomous — they know when to act and when to stop and ask for permission. That balance between autonomy and safety is what makes this practical for real use.

Built with Claude Code, Obsidian, Python, Playwright, and a lot of late nights 🌙
